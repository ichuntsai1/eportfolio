Welcome to I-Chun Tsai's Portfolio

I, I-Chun Tsai, who is originally from Taipei, Taiwan received my Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration in June 1998 and Master of Educational Technology in January 2000. Additionally, my Ph.D. was completed in the School of Information Science and Learning Technologies (SISLT), University of Missouri-Columbia in May 2008. When pursuing my Ph.D. in SISLT, I served an instructor and mentor in the Zone for about 3 years. Also, I have served as a research assistant on the CANS Prototype Design Project since Jan. 2007. Currently, I am an instructor teaching in the instructional technology program of the Department of Educational Foundations and Leadership at University of Akron, Ohio.

My research interests have been focused on social computing, online learning community of practice, online learning experience, computer supported collaborative learning (CSCL), computer mediated communication (CMC), and teacher education. Since 2000, I have been participated in my projects regarding to Instructional Technologies. The most current project that I serve as a primary researcher is the Context-aware Activity Notification System (CANS) Prototype Design Project in the School of Information Science and Learning Technologies, University of Missouri-Columbia.

This professional portfolio will demonstrate what I have learned and accomplished as a graduate assistant at University of Missouri-Columbia and as a faculty member at University of Akron. More detail of my research and teaching experiences will be presented in the sections below.
